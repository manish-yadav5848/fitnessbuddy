12-Week Progressive Overload Phases

Phase 1 Foundation Weeks 1–4
  • Learn movements with proper form
  • RPE 6–7 (2–3 reps in reserve)
  • 10–12 reps compounds, 12–15 accessories
  • Add 1–2.5 kg weekly to compounds
  • Zone 2 cardio: 30 min
  • Week 4: DELOAD
Phase 2 Building Weeks 5–8
  • Progressive loading & weak point focus
  • RPE 7–8 (1–2 reps in reserve)
  • 8–10 reps compounds, 10–12 accessories
  • Add 1 set to primary compounds
  • Zone 2 cardio: 35–40 min (treadmill/bike)
  • Week 8: DELOAD
Phase 3 Peak Weeks 9–12
  • Push limits & test new PRs
  • RPE 8–9 (0–1 reps in reserve)
  • 5–8 reps compounds, 8–12 accessories
  • 4 sets compounds, 3 accessories
  • Zone 2 cardio: 40–45 min (treadmill/bike)
  • Week 12: DELOAD

What is Zone 2?

Zone 2 should feel "too easy" at first — that's correct. You must be able to hold a full conversation. Benefits come from consistency and volume at this intensity, not going faster.

Mode Treadmill incline walk (8–12%), stationary bike, or early AM outdoor jog (before 6:30 AM only in summer)
Duration Wk 1–4: 30 min → Wk 5–8: 35 min → Wk 9–12: 40–45 min
Goal Build aerobic base, mitochondrial density, fat oxidation
Test If you can't talk comfortably, you're going too hard

Warm-Up & Cool-Down Protocol

🔥 Warm-Up (10–12 min)

RAISE (3–5 min)

Light cardio: incline walk, rowing, or cycling to 100–120 bpm

ACTIVATE & MOBILIZE (5 min)

Upper days: Arm circles, band pull-aparts, cat-cow, wall slides, scapular push-ups

Lower days: Leg swings, bodyweight squats, hip circles, walking lunges, glute bridges

POTENTIATE (2–3 min)

2 progressive warm-up sets: Set 1 @ 50% × 8 reps → Set 2 @ 75% × 5 reps

⚠️ NO static stretching before lifting — reduces power by 5–8%

❄️ Cool-Down (8–10 min)

LOWER HR (3 min)

Slow walking or light cycling

STATIC STRETCHES (5–7 min)

Quad stretch, hamstring stretch, shoulder stretch, chest stretch, hip flexor lunge, child's pose, spinal twist — 30s each side

🏡 Daily Home Core & Pelvic Routine 10–12 min

Since gym floor space can be cramped, perform these floor-based stability and breathing movements at home (e.g., first thing in the morning upon waking, or right before bed). Focus on deep, controlled movement and proper activation.

1

Diaphragmatic Breathing ▶️

Lie on your back, hands on belly. Expand abdomen on inhale, release on exhale.

10 deep breaths Focus: Relaxation
2

Dead Bug (Core & Pelvic Stability) ▶️

Keep lower back flat. Exhale, draw lower belly in, and engage pelvic floor as you extend limbs.

3 × 8–10 / side 60s rest
3

Glute Bridges (Pelvic Focus) ▶️

Drive through heels. Contract pelvic floor at the top of the bridge, fully relax at the bottom.

3 × 12 reps 60s rest
4

Bird-Dogs (Rotational Stability) ▶️

Extend opposite arm/leg. Exhale and lift the pelvic floor as you extend; keep hips square.

3 × 10 / side 60s rest
5

Happy Baby Stretch & Belly Breaths ▶️

Cool-down stretch. Fully release and open the pelvic floor and hips while taking deep belly breaths.

2 × 45 sec Focus: Release

Supplements

Evidence-based essentials — priority tiers with Indian brand recommendations

🥇 Priority Tier — Highly Recommended

Creatine Monohydrate

₹300-500/mo
5g/day — any time, daily

Most researched supplement for strength & muscle. Vegetarians respond even better due to lower baseline creatine stores.

Brands: Fast&Up, MuscleBlaze, ON

Vitamin D3

₹200-400/mo
1,500-2,000 IU/day — with fat-containing meal

70-80% of Indians are deficient. Essential for muscle function, immunity, mood. Get blood levels tested.

Brands: HealthKart, Carbamide Forte

Vitamin B12

₹200-300/mo
500-1,000 mcg/day — morning

Eggs provide some B12, but supplementation ensures adequacy. Critical for energy and nerve health.

Brands: HealthKart, Carbamide Forte

Omega-3 (Algal Oil)

₹500-800/mo
500-1,000 mg EPA+DHA — with meals

Vegetarian omega-3 source. Reduces inflammation, supports recovery. Flaxseeds provide ALA but conversion to EPA/DHA is poor (~5%).

Brands: Boldfit, Wellbeing Nutrition
Total Priority Tier: ₹1,200–2,000/month

🚨 Signs You Need an Unplanned Deload

⚠️ Lifts feeling 20%+ heavier than normal for 2+ sessions
🔴 Persistent joint pain (not just DOMS)
😴 Poor sleep quality for 3+ consecutive nights
💓 Elevated resting heart rate (5+ bpm above baseline)
😤 Irritability, loss of motivation, brain fog

🔄 Deload Week Protocol (Weeks 4, 8, 12)

Lifting weightReduce to 50–60% of working weights
SetsReduce by 50% (4 sets → 2 sets)
Cardio volumeReduce by 30% (40 min → 28 min)
HIITReplace with Zone 2 or skip entirely
FrequencyMaintain normal schedule
FocusPerfect form, mobility, sleep, nutrition

⚠️ Important Disclaimers

  • Consult a physician before beginning any new exercise regimen
  • If you experience sharp joint pain (not DOMS), stop and consult a professional
  • For pelvic floor: if no improvement after 4-6 weeks, see a pelvic floor physiotherapist
  • Start conservatively — ego is the #1 cause of injury for returning trainees
  • Track weight weekly (same time, same conditions) and adjust calories accordingly
  • If new to high protein, increase gradually over 2 weeks to avoid digestive issues
